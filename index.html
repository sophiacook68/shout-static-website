Understanding the Proposal

What is the HVCTS?

The Homeowner Vehicle & Council Tax Surcharge (HVCTS) is a proposed additional levy targeting property owners across England and Wales. It seeks to impose a surcharge on top of existing council tax obligations, linked to vehicle ownership or property characteristics.

Proponents argue the measure will generate revenue to fund local infrastructure and public services. However, the proposal raises serious questions about fairness, proportionality, and the additional pressure it places on homeowners already facing the rising cost of living.

This campaign is not opposed to funding public services. We are opposed to a policy that is poorly designed, disproportionately burdensome, and lacks proper democratic scrutiny.

Key Facts

▶

Applies as a surcharge on top of existing council tax bills

▶

Affects property owners who also own or operate a vehicle

▶

No independent impact assessment has been published

▶

Applies regardless of income, financial hardship, or need

▶

No equivalent charge on renters or commercial leaseholders

Our Position

Why We Oppose the HVCTS

01

It Is Fundamentally Unfair

The surcharge targets homeowners exclusively, creating a two-tier system that penalises property ownership. Renters in equivalent circumstances face no comparable burden.

02

It Lacks Proportionality

The levy does not account for income, household composition, or genuine need. A retired couple on fixed income faces the same charge as a high-earning household.

03

No Democratic Mandate

This policy was not prominently featured in any election manifesto. It represents a significant tax increase introduced without a clear public mandate or national debate.

04

No Independent Assessment

The government has not published an independent impact assessment examining the financial and social effects on homeowners across the income spectrum.

05

Chilling Effect on Homeownership

Imposing further costs on property owners discourages homeownership — a goal successive governments have stated they wish to support and expand.

06

Double Taxation

Homeowners already pay council tax and vehicle excise duty. The HVCTS effectively creates a third levy on the same assets without delivering any additional service.
